# Akari — Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** 14 July 2026
**App:** Akari (明かり) — a personal energy diary for ME/CFS
**Contact:** mazlabz.ai@gmail.com

## The short version

Akari keeps everything on your phone. It has no account, no cloud, no analytics, no ads, and **no internet permission at all** — the app is technically incapable of sending your data anywhere.

## What Akari stores, and where

Everything you log — morning check-ins, activities, rest, symptoms, vitals, notes, and PEM (crash) flags — is saved **only in Akari's private storage on your device** (a local database and a local settings file). None of it is transmitted off the device, because Akari cannot access the internet.

Akari does **not** collect, and has no ability to receive:

- your name or contact details (an optional first name you may enter stays on the device),
- your location,
- advertising or analytics identifiers,
- crash/usage telemetry.

## Health Connect (optional)

If — and only if — you choose to connect Health Connect, Akari requests **read-only** access to a single data type: **heart rate**. It uses your latest heart-rate reading to show whether you are near your personal pacing ceiling. This reading is displayed live and is **not written anywhere off your device**. Akari never writes to Health Connect, and requests no other health data types. You can disconnect at any time in Settings → Health Connect, or revoke access in the Health Connect app.

## Sharing

Akari shares your data with **no one**. When you use "Export for doctor (CSV)" or "Back up everything (JSON)", *you* choose a destination file through your device's own file picker; Akari writes the file there and takes no further action. Where that file goes next is entirely under your control.

## Retention and deletion

Your data stays until you remove it. You can delete everything at any time in **Settings → Clear all data**, or by uninstalling the app. Both permanently erase Akari's local data from your device.

## Children

Akari is a general-audience wellbeing tool and is not directed at children. It knowingly collects no personal information from anyone, including children.

## Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new effective date.

## Not medical advice

Akari is a personal diary, not a medical device and not a source of medical advice. Always consult a qualified clinician about your health.
